About Emerald Luxner:

I am an aspiring game programmer who makes original video games while also making my own mods for video games that I play. I am a sophomore in college at Schenectady County Community College pursuing a degree in programming in game design. I have taken 5 classes so far (C#, 2 English classes, Java, and Psychology) and plan to take 1 Algebra class in the summer and 3 other classes (Unity, Another Algebra, and Black History) in the fall.

I have great time management skills and make To-Do lists and Schedule my day to manage between college, work, and personal life. I am also great with co-workers or classmates. I have worked on 4 group projects so far in my career with multiple different kinds of people and plan to do one more in the summer.

Game development:

I found my love for programming when I was in 5th grade during the computer science unit at my school. During the unit, we used a website called Code.org, a website that teaches kids how to code using blocks (a version of javascript). During that unit all I could talk about was programming but, I couldn’t code on my tablet at the time. So that Christmas, my dad bought me a cheap computer so I could practice.

I joined a 2 year Multimedia and gaming program so I could learn more about coding and get experience. I learned many game development skills like programming, audio development, art, and game design and writing. I learned how to program using Construct 3 and Godot and make websites (like the one you are using) with CSS, HTML, and Java. I learned and applied fundamental concepts of 3D game programming like scene graphs, meshes, colliders, cameras, and lighting. I learned how to implement common 3D game features. I learned audio production using ChipTone, Bosca Ceoil, REAPER, MuseScore, and Audacity. I created music, sound effects, and edited a voice actor’s voice. I tried out 2d and 3d art (using Blender, Krita, and Piskel), I learned and applied fundamental skills of 3D digital art and animation including modeling, texturing, rigging, and sculpting. I created characters and environments for games.I learned the MDA framework, design pillars, prototyping, and playtesting, as well as methods for communicating design ideas. I learned about how to apply fundamental narrative concepts within the context of games and interactive fiction. I practiced creating branching narratives and environmental storytelling using accessible authoring tools like Ren’Py and Bitsy.

I have worked on a total of 5 game projects, Bellaby’s Dungeon Escape, Lovin’ Hell, ChatBot, Natalie Vs. The Mind, and a new unnamed game based on bees. My first game project was ChatBot which I made in fall 2024 using construct 3. I had never made game art or game coding before so it was a huge learning curve. My first game project with other people was Lovin’ Hell which was made with one of my friends who was the artist while I coded the game. There is more about these games in the Programming tab.

I’m also doing the “Very serious game jam” in June with someone I have never collaborated with before. We are making a 3d game using Godot and Blender.

My goals for the future is to meet other game developers by going to local meetups and talking to people online. I have already achieved some of this goal by meeting other game development students at my college. Another goal of mine is to someday have my own game company that makes life sims. I plan to achieve this goal by making a couple life simulation games similar to the sims since there aren't many competitors.
